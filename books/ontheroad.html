On the Road by Jack Kerouac is a seminal work of the Beat Generation, chronicling the free-spirited journeys of Sal Paradise, a young writer, and his charismatic friend Dean Moriarty.
Set against the backdrop of post-war America, the novel captures their adventures as they travel across the country in search of freedom, self-discovery, and meaning.
Written in a spontaneous and energetic style, the book is a celebration of jazz, poetry, and the countercultural spirit of the 1950s, offering a vivid exploration of friendship, rebellion, and the open road.
